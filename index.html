The Thesis

A loan-to-own play on a fully-improved Jackson Square office building.

Ares originated a $54.9M loan on the building in 2019 against a $70.5M purchase. Pendulum is acquiring that loan at $47.25M — roughly 88¢ on the dollar of the original origination, and ±57% of the current owner's all-in basis. The building is already $1,019/sf into capital that we don't have to spend.

Three things make the deal work at this basis:

  1. Jackson Square is the strongest sub-market in SF. Vacancy in the low-20s vs. 32.8% across the broader CBD. Consistent positive net absorption. Zero new supply in pipeline thanks to zoning constraints.
  2. The asset is fully improved. Historic creative office, fully renovated in 2014 with subsequent capital from the current owner. Above-market ceiling heights, massive windows, reimagined lobby, on-site coffee shop. We're not chasing capex.
  3. Strong in-place cash flow with mark-to-market upside. ±75% occupied with a 4.5-year WALT across Minted, BraunHagey & Borden, IVP, and Empower Gym. Untrended mark-to-market yield of 8% and stabilized yield on cost of ±10%.
There is a somewhat predictable swing of the pendulum in real estate investing. It requires creativity, deep analytical aptitude, experience and conviction to unlock value when fear grips the marketplace. — Kevin Hayes, CEO, Pendulum Property Partners
